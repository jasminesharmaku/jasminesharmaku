Shatabdhi Ghar

A Century-Old Legacy Reimagined

About Shatabdhi Ghar

Shatabdhi Ghar, meaning "century house" in Nepali, is a beautifully preserved 100-year-old traditional Nepali home located in the heart of Kushma. This architectural gem serves as the headquarters of the Kushma Art Project and stands as a living testament to Nepal's rich cultural heritage.

The Space

The house features traditional Nepali craftsmanship with intricately carved wooden windows, clay-tiled roofs, and spacious courtyards. We've carefully restored and adapted the space to serve multiple functions:

  • Art Gallery & Exhibition Space
  • Artist Studios & Workrooms
  • Community Meeting Hall
  • Library & Resource Center
  • Kitchen & Dining Area
  • Guest Rooms for Resident Artists
  • Outdoor Performance Area
  • Traditional Nepali Garden

Current Needs

To continue serving our growing community, we urgently need to fund the renovation and equipment of the community kitchen. This space will enable us to host more workshops, feed resident artists, and provide cooking classes to local youth.
